>
> I'm writing on behalf of Faresay, an online marketplace that connects clients with licensed
> mental-health professionals (psychologists, clinical social workers, and counsellors). We
> operate in the UK today and are planning a US launch with the goal of being properly authorised
> to operate across all states.
>
> We're seeking healthcare-regulatory counsel with multi-state, **behavioral-health** experience
> to advise on our operating structure. The questions most on our mind:
>
>  • Corporate practice (of medicine *and* of psychology/behavioral health) and fee-splitting:
>    our marketplace currently charges a 15% platform fee, and we want the structure — and how
>    that fee is characterised — to be compliant across states, including an MSO / friendly-PC
>    model where required, and a per-state view of where a PC is actually needed.
>  • US entity structure and the path to qualifying in each state.
>  • Clinician licensing across states and use of interstate compacts (PSYPACT, Counseling
>    Compact, Social Work Licensure Compact).
>  • HIPAA and state health-privacy obligations, and telehealth compliance.
>
> Could you let us know (1) whether this is within your practice and your relevant multi-state
> behavioral-health experience; (2) how you'd suggest scoping an initial engagement — we'd favour
> a fixed-fee scoping phase to start; and (3) your fee structure? Happy to share a short business
> overview and find a time to talk.
>
> With thanks,
> Joel Harvey — Faresay — legal@faresay.com

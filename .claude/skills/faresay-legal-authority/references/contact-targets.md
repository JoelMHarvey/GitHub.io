# Contact Targets — who to reach, in what order

The agent identifies specific, current contacts via WebSearch (don't rely on memory for names,
fees, or availability) and drafts outreach for Joel's approval. Order reflects leverage: the
right attorney first, because that decision shapes everything else.

## 1. US healthcare-regulatory counsel  ← highest priority
**What to look for:** firms/attorneys in **digital health / telehealth / behavioral-health
regulatory** law with demonstrated **multi-state CPOM and fee-splitting** experience and the
**MSO / friendly-PC** model for therapy/mental-health platforms.
- Sources: digital-health practice groups at national firms; telehealth-focused boutiques;
  referrals from health-tech founder communities; state bar referral services.
- First contact: intro + scoping inquiry (`templates/attorney-inquiry.md`). Ask for **scope,
  approach to the 15%-fee/CPOM question, multi-state experience, and fee structure** (prefer a
  fixed-fee scoping engagement to start).
- Goal: shortlist 3–5, compare, Joel engages one.

## 2. Registered-agent & entity-formation / compliance services
**What:** a provider that can serve as **registered agent in all 50 states + DC** and handle
foreign qualifications and annual reports under one account (most cost-efficient for "all 50").
- Examples of the category: national registered-agent/compliance providers (CSC, Cт
  Corporation, Cogency/COGENCY GLOBAL, Harbor Compliance, Northwest, LegalZoom, etc. — get
  current quotes; do not assume pricing).
- First contact: quote request (`templates/registered-agent-inquiry.md`) — per-state vs
  all-states bundle pricing, what's included (formation, foreign qualification, annual reports).

## 3. State licensing boards & regulators (clarification only)
**What:** when counsel needs a specific state's position confirmed (e.g. out-of-state telehealth
registration, a board's stance on the marketplace model).
- First contact: a precise, polite written inquiry (`templates/state-board-inquiry.md`).
- Note: boards give general guidance, not legal advice — route their answers back to counsel.

## 4. Compliance / privacy vendors
- HIPAA compliance tooling, BAA management, security assessments.
- Provider-license **verification & monitoring** services (so Faresay continuously confirms each
  clinician is licensed in the client's state).
- Engage only after counsel defines requirements (Phase 3).

## 5. Insurance brokers
- Professional liability (for clinicians), **tech/cyber E&O** (for the platform).
- Get quotes; never bind coverage without approval.

## Contact hygiene (applies to all)

- Verify the organisation and the person before sending (official site, reputation-check the
  domain/email/phone with Malwarebytes).
- One clear ask per message; professional signature as Faresay.
- Track every contact, thread, quote, and next-step date in `assets/state-tracker.csv` /
  `assets/decisions-log.md`.
- Persistent-but-polite follow-up: a gentle nudge after ~5–7 business days of silence, then
  space out; never more than a couple of follow-ups without a new reason to write.

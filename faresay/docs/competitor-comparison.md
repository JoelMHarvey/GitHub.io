**Internal · v1.0 · June 2026.** Candid positioning vs the practice-management tools a UK private-practice therapist actually considers. Facts web-researched + fact-checked June 2026 — competitor pricing/features change, so re-verify before quoting externally.

> **Honest headline:** Faresay is the **newest and simplest** option, **free to start**, **UK-therapist-first**, with the essentials **all included** and the therapist owning everything. The incumbents are **more mature and feature-rich** (group practice, insurance/claims, ePrescribe, deep clinical tooling, native apps) and mostly **paid-only**. We win on **calm + fairness + free entry**, not breadth.

## Per-competitor read

**SimplePractice** — the US incumbent (100k+ providers), deepest feature set: insurance/claims, ePrescribe, measurement-based care, strong native apps, free directory. **But fundamentally US**: USD-only, US data residency, HIPAA-not-GDPR, US-bank payouts — UK private practice fights the defaults the whole way. *Our angle: we're the UK-native answer; they aren't built for here.*

**WriteUpp** — genuine UK peer: UK support, GBP, EU data residency, processor stance, no cut. Mature, allied-health-broad. SMS is credit-based; UI is more "clinical software" than calm. *Our angle: free to start, simpler, therapist-warm, outcome measures + self-booking native.*

**Halaxy** — free core is compelling, global, EU residency for EU users. But it runs a **client-facing marketplace/directory** and monetises via paid SMS/telehealth credits + payment fees; export has friction. *Our angle: free too, but UK-focused, calmer, and we don't muddle software with a marketplace.*

**Power Diary / Zanda** — strong UK posture (UK data, GBP), cheap entry (£15), no cut, mature. Allied-health generalist, busier UI. *Our angle: therapist-specific (not generalist), free entry, simpler, mental-health tooling (outcome measures) built in.*

**Cliniko** — excellent UK data residency + explicit "you are the controller" GDPR stance; loved for reliability. £59 entry is the priciest here and it's allied-health-generalist, not therapy-shaped. *Our angle: free to start vs £59, therapy-specific, calmer.*

**Carepatron** — free plan + lots of features + AI notes, but **US/HIPAA-centric**, USD pricing, weaker UK-specific residency. *Our angle: UK-native + GBP + UK GDPR.*

## Where Faresay genuinely wins (lead with these)
1. **Free to start.** Starter £0 — only Halaxy/Carepatron also free, and they're not UK-therapist-first. WriteUpp/Cliniko/Zanda/SimplePractice are paid-only.
2. **Built for UK therapists, calmly.** Therapy-shaped, not allied-health-generalist; opinionated simplicity; phone-first; set up in ~15 min.
3. **Everything a solo UK therapist needs, included** — secure video, GBP payments + payouts, email **and** SMS reminders, intake/consent, **outcome measures**, self-booking — without per-feature add-ons or SMS credit packs.
4. **You own it all.** Therapist = data controller; export and leave anytime. Processor DPA, UK/EU residency, ICO.
5. **Fair + transparent.** Free entry, pay-as-you-earn commission that drops to 0% — no marketplace cut on your practice.

## Where we're honestly behind (don't hide; address in roadmap)
- **Maturity/breadth:** incumbents have years of features — insurance/claims, ePrescribe, deep reporting, group-practice tooling.
- **Native app:** we're a PWA today (installs to home screen); native app is on the roadmap (Capacitor).
- **Card commission:** we add 0–2.5% on card payments; pure-subscription rivals don't. Framed honestly, it's the price of the **free** entry tier — and it's 0% on Clinic.
- **Track record:** we're new. Trust is earned; the incumbents have it.

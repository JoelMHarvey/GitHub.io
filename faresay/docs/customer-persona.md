## Primary persona — "Sarah" (the one we build for first)

**Sarah, 34 · British · living in Tokyo · corporate professional**

- **Situation:** relocated ~18 months ago for work. Competent and busy on the outside; isolated
  underneath. Friends and family are 8 time zones away. Doesn't speak enough Japanese to navigate
  local mental-health services, and isn't sure they'd "get" her anyway.
- **What she's feeling:** loneliness, low-level anxiety, identity wobble ("who am I here?"), strain
  on a long-distance or cross-cultural relationship, pressure to seem fine.
- **What she wants:** to talk to someone **in English** who **understands expat life** — without
  flying home, without a months-long waitlist, without explaining the basics of being an outsider.
- **Trigger:** a bad week. She opens Google at 11pm and types **"English speaking therapist Tokyo"**
  or **"online therapy for expats."**
- **Objections / friction:** *Is this person actually qualified? Will they understand my situation?
  What's the time difference for sessions? How much, and can I cancel? Is my data private?*
- **Where she spends time:** Google, expat **Facebook groups** ("Tokyo Expats", "British in Japan"),
  **Reddit** (r/Tokyo, r/expats, r/japanlife), LinkedIn, Instagram.
- **Why Faresay wins her:** the page she lands on is **about her life**, the therapist is **verified
  and English-speaking with cross-cultural experience**, booking shows **her timezone**, the price is
  clear, and someone **replies quickly**. Trust, fit, no friction.

**Her one-line job-to-be-done:** *"Help me find a therapist who understands what it's like to live
here as a foreigner — quickly, in English, that I can trust."*

## Secondary persona — "Marco" (breadth check)

**Marco, 41 · Italian · living in Singapore · senior tech/finance**

- **Situation:** high earner, high stress, frequent mover (3rd country in 8 years). Burnout building;
  marriage strained by relocations; doesn't want a colleague to know he's struggling.
- **Wants:** discreet, professional, English-speaking therapy that fits a packed calendar across
  time zones; quality over price.
- **Searches:** "confidential online therapy", "executive stress therapist", "therapy for expats Singapore".
- **Why Faresay:** privacy + verified quality + timezone-aware booking; price is not the barrier —
  **trust and fit** are. (Validates the higher expat price point in `financial-model.md`.)

## Secondary persona — "Aisha" (corridor breadth)

**Aisha, 29 · British-Pakistani · living in Dubai · marketing professional**

- **Situation:** moved for a great job; loves it but feels culturally between worlds; family pressure
  back home; few people she can be fully open with.
- **Wants:** a therapist who understands **identity, belonging and bicultural life** — culturally
  sensitive, English-speaking, non-judgemental.
- **Searches:** "therapist who understands cultural identity", "English therapist Dubai", "online
  therapy expats UAE".
- **Why Faresay:** the positioning ("therapy that understands international life") and the ability to
  filter for a therapist with relevant cultural/identity experience.

## How to use these personas
- **Channels:** go where they actually are (§3 of `gtm-strategy.md`) — their Facebook groups,
  subreddits, search terms.
- **Messaging:** write to Sarah's 11pm feeling, not to "the mental-health market."
- **Product:** the objections above are a backlog — verified badges, timezone display, clear pricing,
  fast response, privacy assurances (see `product-requirements-document.md`).
- **SEO:** their literal searches are your landing-page titles.
- **Refine:** replace these archetypes with **real client interviews** as soon as you have clients.

Faresay Data Processing Agreement

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") forms part of, and is incorporated into, the Faresay Therapist Agreement and the Terms of Service (together, the "Agreement") between Faresay and the therapist, practitioner or practice that subscribes to the Faresay platform (the "Customer").

It governs the processing of personal data carried out by Faresay on the Customer's behalf and is designed to satisfy Article 28 of the UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018.

Roles. Under this DPA the Customer is the Controller of the clinical and client personal data they manage on the platform, and Faresay is the Processor, acting only on the Customer's documented instructions. Faresay is an independent Controller only for the limited account-administration, billing and security data described in Annex 1. This split reflects Faresay's product model: the therapist owns the client relationship and the clinical record; Faresay provides the software.

1. Definitions

Terms not defined here have the meaning given in the UK GDPR. In this DPA:

  • "UK GDPR" means Regulation (EU) 2016/679 as it forms part of UK law by virtue of the European Union (Withdrawal) Act 2018, together with the Data Protection Act 2018.
  • "Controller", "Processor", "Data Subject", "Personal Data", "Special Category Data", "Processing", "Personal Data Breach" have the meanings in the UK GDPR.
  • "Customer Personal Data" means Personal Data that Faresay Processes on the Customer's behalf under the Agreement, as described in Annex 1.
  • "Sub-processor" means any third party engaged by Faresay to Process Customer Personal Data. The current list is at Annex 3 (Sub-processor List).
  • "TOMs" means the technical and organisational measures at Annex 2 (Technical & Organisational Measures).

2. Subject matter, duration, nature and purpose

Item Detail
Subject matter Provision of the Faresay practice-management platform to the Customer.
Duration For the term of the Agreement, plus any post-termination period in Section 11.
Nature Hosting, storage, transmission, scheduling, payment facilitation, video session hosting, messaging, reminders and related processing operations.
Purpose To enable the Customer to run their therapy practice (manage clients, book and deliver sessions, take payment, send reminders).
Categories of Data Subject The Customer's clients; the Customer's own staff/associates where applicable.
Categories of Personal Data Identity and contact details; appointment and scheduling data; payment metadata; messages; client-supplied intake responses; special-category health data (mental-health information).

The full detail is set out in Annex 1.

3. Processing only on documented instructions

3.1 Faresay shall Process Customer Personal Data only on the documented instructions of the Customer, including as set out in the Agreement, this DPA, and as given through the platform's features, unless required to do otherwise by UK law (in which case Faresay shall, where legally permitted, inform the Customer first).

3.2 Faresay shall immediately inform the Customer if, in its opinion, an instruction infringes the UK GDPR or other data-protection law.

3.3 Faresay shall not sell Customer Personal Data, nor use it for its own purposes, profiling, marketing, or to train AI models.

4. Confidentiality

Faresay shall ensure that persons authorised to Process Customer Personal Data are bound by an appropriate duty of confidentiality and Process the data only as instructed. Access is granted on a least-privilege, need-to-know basis (see Annex 2).

5. Security (Article 32)

5.1 Faresay shall implement and maintain the technical and organisational measures in Annex 2, appropriate to the risk, taking into account the special-category nature of the data.

5.2 Faresay may update the TOMs from time to time provided the level of protection is not materially reduced.

6. Sub-processors

6.1 The Customer grants general authorisation for Faresay to engage the Sub-processors listed in Annex 3.

6.2 Faresay shall impose data-protection obligations on each Sub-processor that are no less protective than those in this DPA, by written contract, and remains fully liable to the Customer for each Sub-processor's performance.

6.3 Faresay shall give the Customer at least 14 days' notice (by email and/or in-product) before adding or replacing a Sub-processor. The Customer may object on reasonable data-protection grounds; if the parties cannot resolve the objection, the Customer may terminate the affected service.

7. Data-subject rights

Taking into account the nature of the Processing, Faresay shall assist the Customer by appropriate technical and organisational measures, insofar as possible, to fulfil the Customer's obligation to respond to requests to exercise Data Subject rights (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection). Where a Data Subject contacts Faresay directly about Customer Personal Data, Faresay shall, without undue delay, refer them to the Customer.

8. Personal Data Breach

8.1 Faresay shall notify the Customer without undue delay, and in any event within 48 hours, after becoming aware of a Personal Data Breach affecting Customer Personal Data.

8.2 The notification shall describe, to the extent known: the nature of the breach; categories and approximate number of Data Subjects and records affected; likely consequences; and measures taken or proposed. Faresay shall provide reasonable assistance to the Customer in meeting the Customer's own breach-notification duties to the ICO and Data Subjects (UK GDPR Arts 33–34).

9. DPIAs and prior consultation

Faresay shall provide the Customer with reasonable assistance with data-protection impact assessments (Art 35) and prior consultation with the ICO (Art 36), taking into account the nature of Processing and the information available to Faresay. A platform-level DPIA is maintained at Data Protection Impact Assessment.

10. International transfers

10.1 Faresay primarily stores Customer Personal Data on infrastructure located in the UK / EEA (see Annex 3).

10.2 Where any transfer of Customer Personal Data to a third country occurs (for example via a Sub-processor), Faresay shall ensure an appropriate safeguard is in place — the UK International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA), the UK Addendum to the EU Standard Contractual Clauses, or an adequacy regulation — together with any supplementary measures required following a transfer risk assessment.

11. Return and deletion

11.1 On termination of the Agreement, Faresay shall, at the Customer's choice, return or delete all Customer Personal Data, and delete existing copies, unless UK law requires storage.

11.2 The Customer may export their data at any time during the term via the platform's export features. Faresay shall make Customer Personal Data available for export for 30 days after termination, after which it shall be deleted within a further 90 days, subject to routine backup-rotation cycles.

12. Audit

12.1 Faresay shall make available to the Customer all information reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with Article 28 and this DPA.

12.2 Faresay shall allow for and contribute to audits, including inspections, conducted by the Customer or an auditor it mandates, on reasonable prior notice (at least 30 days), no more than once per 12-month period (save where required by a supervisory authority or following a breach), subject to confidentiality. Faresay may satisfy audit requests by providing its security documentation, the TOMs, and responses to a reasonable security questionnaire.

13. Liability and precedence

13.1 The liability provisions of the Agreement apply to this DPA.

13.2 In the event of conflict between this DPA and the rest of the Agreement on data-protection matters, this DPA prevails.

14. Governing law

This DPA is governed by the laws of England and Wales and subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of its courts. England and Wales (solicitor to confirm any Scotland/NI variation if serving those nations)

Annex 1 — Details of Processing

  • Controller: the Customer (therapist / practice).
  • Processor: Faresay Ltd, a company registered in England and Wales (company number 17302034), registered office 167-169 Great Portland Street, London W1W 5PF.
  • Subject matter, duration, nature, purpose: as Section 2.
  • Categories of Data Subject: the Customer's clients; the Customer's staff/associates where applicable.
  • Categories of Personal Data: name, email, phone; date of birth (optional); appointment dates/times; session/booking history; payment metadata (card details are handled by Stripe — Faresay does not store card numbers); messages between Customer and client; client intake/consent responses; special-category health data (mental-health information the Customer or client records).
  • Special-category data: Yes — health data. Processed because it is necessary for the provision of health/social-care services by a health professional under a duty of confidentiality (UK GDPR Art 9(2)(h)) and/or with explicit consent (Art 9(2)(a)), as determined by the Controller.
  • Faresay-as-Controller data (outside this DPA): the Customer's own account/login credentials, subscription and billing records, platform audit logs, aggregate/cookieless usage analytics, and security telemetry — Processed by Faresay as an independent Controller under the Privacy Policy.

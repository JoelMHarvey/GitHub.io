1. Beachhead customer

English-speaking expatriates, 25–55, living overseas, paying privately, seeking therapy for stress, isolation, relationships, or cultural adjustment.

  • First corridor (pick ONE to start): the city/country where the founder's network and presence are strongest — candidate: Tokyo / Japan (founder is based there). One corridor, proven, before a second. [PLACEHOLDER: confirm first corridor.]
  • Who they are in detail: see customer-persona.md (Sarah, Tokyo — the persona the team returns to when marketing gets hard).
  • Why them: underserved for culturally-fluent English-language therapy, naturally cash-pay, concentrated and reachable online, founder-fit.

2. Positioning

"Therapy that understands international life."

  • One-liner: Faresay helps English-speaking people abroad find a trusted therapist who gets expat life — relocation, isolation, identity, belonging.
  • Message pillars: (1) cross-cultural understanding, (2) trust — every therapist verified, licensed, interviewed, (3) English-speaking & culturally fluent, (4) timezone- friendly booking, (5) fair, transparent price.
  • Not: a generic global therapy app; not crisis care; not "cheapest."

4. CAC assumptions (tie to financial-model.md)

  • Stage-0 ceiling: blended CAC < £100. Aim materially lower via organic/referral.
  • Why low CAC is non-negotiable: at an expat price of £120 × 15% ≈ £18/session (~£14.20 after processing), CAC must stay under ~£57 to pay back within 4 sessions (Stage-0 target). An organic-heavy mix is the only way the 15% take pays back fast.
  • Funnel metrics to track per channel: cost per lead (CPL) → cost per booked consultation → CAC → month-2 retention. A channel earns more budget only when its CAC + conversion clear the bar.

5. First-year growth — the 100 → 1,000 → 10,000 motion

First 100 (Stage 0, ~6 months): one corridor, founder-led. Seed a small verified therapist panel → publish corridor/intent SEO pages → show up helpfully in 3–5 expat communities → manual outreach to relocation contacts → first bookings. Gate: hit Stage 0 (100 paying clients, CAC < £100, payback ≤ ~4 sessions, retention > 60% m2) before opening a second corridor.

First 1,000: replicate the proven playbook across 2–3 more corridors; scale SEO surface area; turn on the referral flywheel (clients refer clients, therapists refer therapists); begin small, measured paid on the terms that converted; first partnership pilots.

First 10,000: multi-corridor brand for "therapy for globally mobile people"; partnerships at scale (relocation/employers/schools); paid scaled only where unit economics hold; referral + data + reputation moats compounding (see business-plan.md §12).

7. Criteria for success / failure

Per channel (kill or scale): - Scale a channel when its CAC < £100 (ideally < £57), it produces booked consultations, and those clients retain > 60% at month 2. - Kill / pause a channel when CAC stays > £100 after a defined spend/effort window, or it produces leads that don't book or don't retain.

Stage-0 go/no-go (the master gate): proceed to corridor #2 only when, in corridor #1: 100 paying clients · blended CAC < £100 · payback ≤ ~4 sessions · month-2 retention > 60% · 25 verified therapists. If any miss → fix the funnel before spending to scale, don't paper over it with a second market.

8. Monday-morning playbook — getting client #1 this week

  1. Pick the corridor and write it down (e.g. Tokyo). Everything below is for that one corridor.
  2. Confirm 3–5 verified therapists are live and bookable in it (seed the panel; real, verified).
  3. Publish one SEO landing page for the top intent term (e.g. "English-speaking therapist in Tokyo") with the positioning from §2 and a clear booking CTA.
  4. Post genuinely useful content (not an ad) in 3 expat communities for that corridor, answering a real question and mentioning Faresay where relevant.
  5. Publish one LinkedIn post as the founder: why expat mental health is hard and what you're building.
  6. DM 10 warm contacts (relocation agents, community admins, expat friends) asking them to share.
  7. Instrument everything (Plausible + a simple CPL/booking tracker) so you can see what converted.
  8. Reply fast to the first enquiries — response time is the product (trust). Book the first consult.

Faresay Privacy Policy

Faresay is practice-management software for therapists. UK-registered therapists, practitioners and practices subscribe to Faresay to run their own private practice — managing their own client list, scheduling and conducting secure video sessions, sending reminders, and taking card payments. Our customer is the therapist. Faresay is not a marketplace, a directory, a matching or client-acquisition service, or a healthcare provider.

This Privacy Policy explains how Faresay handles personal data in its own right — principally the account, billing, security and website data of the therapists who subscribe to us and the visitors to our website. For a therapist's clients' data (including special-category mental-health information), Faresay acts only as a processor on the therapist's instructions; the therapist is the controller of that data. See Section 1.

Because the software is used to deliver mental-health care, some of the data flowing through it is special-category / sensitive health data. We treat all such data with a correspondingly high standard of confidentiality and security, whichever role we are in.

This policy should be read alongside our Terms of Service, the Faresay Therapist Agreement, the Data Processing Agreement, the Sub-processor List, and our Security & Data Protection Policy.

1. Who we are & our two data-protection roles ⚠️ COUNSEL

Faresay ("Faresay", "we", "us", "our") provides the practice-management software that an independent, UK-registered therapist uses to run their own private practice. The therapist brings their own clients, owns the client relationship and the clinical record, and decides why and how their clients' data is used. Faresay provides the tools.

Faresay holds two distinct roles under UK GDPR, and it matters which one applies to a given piece of data:

  • Faresay as controller. For the therapist's own account, identity, practice, billing, subscription, usage, support and security data — and for data about visitors to our website — Faresay is the controller. This Privacy Policy applies to that data.
  • Faresay as processor. For a therapist's clients' data — including intake responses, scheduling, messages and special-category mental-health information — Faresay is a processor, acting only on the therapist's documented instructions under our Data Processing Agreement (UK GDPR Article 28). The therapist is the controller of that client data.

Faresay does not hold or own the clinical record and does not decide the purposes for which a therapist's clients' data is used.

If you are a client of a therapist who uses Faresay: this is not the right document for you. Please refer to your therapist's own privacy notice, and contact your therapist to exercise your rights over your data. Faresay will assist your therapist as required under the Data Processing Agreement.

2. Scope (UK-first)

This policy applies to Faresay's services as offered in the United Kingdom, governed by the UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018.

It covers our website, the Faresay web and mobile applications, and related services (together, the "Platform" or the "software") in respect of the data for which Faresay is the controller (see Section 1). It does not govern a therapist's processing of their own clients' data — that is the therapist's responsibility as controller, supported by the Data Processing Agreement.

This policy does not cover third-party websites or services we link to, which have their own privacy practices.

9. Your rights

9.1 Therapists and website visitors (Faresay as controller)

Where Faresay is the controller of your data, and subject to applicable law, you have the right to: - access the personal data we hold about you; - request rectification of inaccurate data; - request erasure ("right to be forgotten") in certain circumstances; - restrict or object to certain processing; - data portability; - withdraw consent at any time where processing is based on consent (without affecting prior processing); and - not be subject to solely automated decisions with legal/similarly significant effects (see Section 12).

To exercise these rights, contact us using the details in Section 14. We will respond within the timeframes required by law. ⚠️ COUNSEL — note that some rights (e.g. erasure) may be limited where data must be retained for legal, accounting or regulatory reasons.

9.2 Clients of a therapist

If you are a client of a therapist who uses Faresay, the therapist is the controller of your data. Please contact your therapist to exercise your rights (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection). Faresay, as processor, will assist the therapist in responding, and will refer any request it receives directly to the therapist without undue delay (see DPA Section 7).

11. Children's data

Faresay's customer is a professional therapist, and the software is intended for use by adult therapists running their own practice. Faresay does not knowingly collect personal data about children in its role as controller.

A therapist may, as controller, use the software to support clients who are minors. Where that happens, the therapist is responsible, as controller, for the lawful basis, consent/authorisation and safeguarding obligations relating to those clients; Faresay processes that data only as a processor under the Data Processing Agreement.

12. Automated decision-making

Faresay does not make decisions producing legal or similarly significant effects about you based solely on automated processing without human involvement. The software provides tools to the therapist; clinical decisions are made by the therapist, not by Faresay.

⚠️ COUNSEL — confirm this remains accurate as the product evolves; if any solely-automated significant decisions are introduced, additional UK GDPR Article 22 safeguards and disclosures will be required.

13. Data breaches ⚠️ COUNSEL

We maintain procedures to detect, investigate and respond to personal-data breaches.

  • Faresay as controller. Where a breach affecting controller data is likely to result in a risk to rights and freedoms, we will notify the ICO without undue delay and, where required, within 72 hours of becoming aware, and affected individuals where the breach is high-risk.
  • Faresay as processor. Where a breach affects a therapist's clients' data, Faresay will notify the therapist (the controller) without undue delay, and in any event within 48 hours, and assist the therapist with their own notification duties to the ICO and data subjects (see DPA Section 8).

Our incident-response approach is detailed in the Security & Data Protection Policy.

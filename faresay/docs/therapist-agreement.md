# Faresay Therapist Agreement

This Therapist Agreement (the **"Agreement"**) is entered into between Faresay and the independent mental-health professional or practice that subscribes to the Faresay platform (the **"Therapist"**). It governs the Therapist's subscription to, and use of, the Faresay practice-management software.

By clicking to accept, signing, creating an account, or using the Faresay software, the Therapist agrees to be bound by this Agreement.

## 2. Nature of the Relationship

2.1 **Faresay is practice-management software.** Faresay provides software-as-a-service that an independent professional subscribes to in order to run their own private practice — managing their own Clients, scheduling appointments, holding secure video sessions, sending reminders, taking card payments, and keeping practice records. Faresay is **software**, not a care provider, and **not** a marketplace, directory, matching service, referral service, or client-acquisition service. Faresay does not introduce, source, or supply Clients to the Therapist.

2.2 **The Therapist brings and owns their own Clients.** The Therapist runs their own practice and brings their own Clients to the Software. The professional relationship between the Therapist and each Client is exclusively the Therapist's. The Therapist exercises independent professional clinical judgement and is solely responsible for all clinical decisions. Faresay does not direct, control, or interfere with the Therapist's clinical judgement or the content of Clinical Services, and is not a party to the clinical relationship.

2.3 **The Therapist owns and holds the Clinical Record.** The Therapist is the owner and custodian of the Clinical Record, and is responsible for creating, maintaining, retaining, and securing it in accordance with applicable law and professional obligations. Faresay does not hold the Clinical Record. Faresay processes Client data only as a processor on the Therapist's instructions, as described in clause 9, the Data Processing Agreement, and the Privacy Policy.

2.4 **Independent contractor; no employment or agency.** The Therapist is an independent professional and not an employee, worker, partner, joint venturer, or agent of Faresay, and Faresay is not an employee or agent of the Therapist. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employment, agency, or partnership relationship. The Therapist is responsible for their own working methods, scheduling, fees, and the manner in which Clinical Services are delivered, consistent with applicable professional standards.

2.5 **Characterisation of Faresay's fees.** The Subscription Fee is a **fee for a software-as-a-service product** (the practice-management tools described in clause 2.1). The Processing Commission is a **fee for the card-payment-processing facility within the Software**. Neither is a division, split, share, or cut of the Therapist's professional or clinical fee, nor a marketplace fee, referral fee, or consideration for the introduction of Clients. The Therapist sets their own fees and keeps them, less only the underlying payment-processor fees and the Processing Commission.

## 6. Therapist Obligations

The Therapist must at all times:

6.1 **Standard of care.** Provide Clinical Services with the reasonable skill, care, and diligence expected of a competent professional in the relevant discipline, and in accordance with applicable professional, ethical, and regulatory standards.

6.2 **Scope of practice and competence.** Practise only within the scope of the Therapist's registration, training, and competence, and decline or refer where a Client's needs fall outside that scope (including where in-person or higher-acuity care is clinically indicated).

6.3 **Telehealth compliance.** Comply with all applicable telehealth / remote-consultation laws, professional guidance, and technical-safety requirements when delivering Clinical Services remotely, including verifying the Client's identity at each session as required.

6.4 **Informed consent.** Obtain and document appropriate informed consent for assessment and treatment, including consent to remote delivery, before providing Clinical Services.

6.5 **Confidentiality.** Maintain the confidentiality of Client information in accordance with professional duties of confidence, this Agreement, and applicable data-protection law (clause 9), disclosing only as permitted or required by law or to protect against serious harm.

6.6 **Mandatory reporting and safeguarding.** Comply with all safeguarding, mandatory-reporting, and duty-to-warn obligations applicable in the United Kingdom.

6.7 **Crisis and safeguarding.** Adhere to Faresay's Crisis/Safeguarding Policy (`crisis-safeguarding-policy.md`) and Clinical Governance Policy (`clinical-governance-policy.md`), each incorporated into this Agreement by reference, including escalation and risk-management procedures. Crisis handling, risk assessment, and safeguarding rest with the Therapist as the clinician; the Software is for scheduled, non-urgent care and is not a crisis or emergency service.

6.8 **Record-keeping.** Create, hold, and retain Clinical Records in their own keeping in accordance with applicable law and professional standards for the required retention period, and make them available to the Client (and, where lawfully required, to a continuing clinician) as required.

6.9 **Conduct.** Behave professionally toward Clients and Faresay staff, keep account and profile content accurate, and not make any false, misleading, or guaranteed-outcome claims.

6.10 **Cooperation.** Cooperate with Faresay's reasonable account-security, compliance, and support processes.

9.3 **Client requests.** Where a Client contacts Faresay directly to exercise rights over their data, Faresay will refer them to the Therapist as controller and assist the Therapist as required under the Data Processing Agreement. ## 16. Term, Suspension, and Termination

16.1 **Term.** This Agreement starts when the Therapist accepts it and continues for so long as the Therapist maintains a Subscription, until terminated under this clause 16.

16.2 **Cancellation by the Therapist.** The Therapist may cancel the Subscription at any time through their account. Cancellation takes effect at the end of the then-current paid month, and Subscription Fees already paid for the current month are non-refundable except as required by law.

16.3 **Termination for cause by Faresay.** Faresay may suspend access or terminate immediately on written notice if the Therapist:

16.3.1 loses, or has restricted, any required registration or insurance;
16.3.2 is subject to a regulatory, safeguarding, or fitness-to-practise concern that Faresay reasonably considers presents a risk to Clients;
16.3.3 commits a material breach of this Agreement or the Faresay Policies (and, if remediable, fails to remedy it within [PLACEHOLDER: cure period]);
16.3.4 fails to pay the Subscription Fee or any sum due when due; or
16.3.5 becomes insolvent or ceases to practise.

16.4 **Suspension.** Faresay may suspend the Therapist's access pending investigation of any matter under clause 16.3 where reasonably necessary to protect Clients or the integrity of the Software, acting in good faith.

16.5 **Effect of termination; data export.** On termination, the licence in clause 10.2 ends and the Therapist must stop using the Software and Faresay marks. The Therapist may export their data during the term and for a period after termination, after which Faresay returns or deletes it, in accordance with the Data Processing Agreement. The Therapist remains responsible, as controller, for retaining the Clinical Record for the period their professional standards require. Accrued payment rights, and clauses intended to survive (including clauses 2.3, 5.5–5.6, 7, 9, 11–15, 17, 19, and 20), survive termination.
